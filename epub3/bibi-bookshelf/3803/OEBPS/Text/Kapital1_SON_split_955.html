[111] Gladstone'un Avam Kamarası'nda 13. Şubat 1843 tarihinde yaptığı konuşmadan: "It is one of the most melancholy features in the social state of this country that we see, beyond the possibility of denial, that while there is at this moment a decrease in the consuming powers of the people, an increase of the pressure of privations and distress; there is at the same time a constant accumulation of wealth in the upper classes, an increase in the luxuriousness of their habits, and of their means of enjoyment." ("Times," 14. Feb. 1843. - Hansard, 13. Febr.)
